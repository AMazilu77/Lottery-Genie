Rules & How to win


1. Select the amount you want to play (Pick three numbers between 0-9 from each column for a total of three digits): 50 cents or $1 (all Straight/Box and 1-OFF plays cost $1). Combo plays cost $1.50 to $6.00)

2. There are two drawings per day seven days per week, at approximately 1:30 p.m. and 9:45 p.m. ET. Tickets for each drawing can be purchased up to 13 minutes prior to the applicable drawing – 1:17 p.m. ET for the midday drawings and 9:32 p.m. ET for the evening drawings.

3. Select the draw time. Select MID for the midday drawing (1:30 p.m. ET), EVE for the evening drawing (9:45p.m. ET) draw, or BOTH for both the midday and evening drawings. If no draw time is marked, the terminal will automatically print a ticket for the next available draw. If BOTH is selected, two separate tickets will print – one for the midday drawing and one for the evening drawing.

You can win a top prize of $50 with a $1 Straight play, or increase your chances of winning with a Box, Straight/Box, Front/Back Number or 1-OFF play.

Reminder: Prizes must be claimed within 180 days of the draw date. All PICK 3 prizes may be claimed at any authorized Florida Lottery retailer.

Different ways to win

Florida lottery offers different ways to win and maximize chances of winning prizes. Below the terms are explained:

1. Strait: Match numbers in exact order. 1$ strait play pays off $500.00 ( and 50 cent play pays of $250.00) with odds of 1 : 1000

2. 3-Way Box: Box play means matching numbers in any order for a 3-way prize of $160.00 on a $1.00 play (or $80.000 with a 50 cent play) with odds of 1:333.33

Select a 3-digit number with two like digits and get 3 ways to win. Example, play the numbers "112" and win 3-way box if numbers "112,121,211" come up

3. 6-Way Box: Box play means matching numbers in any order for a 3-way prize of $80.00 on a $1.00 play (or $40.000 with a 50 cent play) with odds of 1:666.67

Select a 3-digit number with all different digits and get 6 ways to win. Example, play the numbers "112" and win 3-way box if numbers "112,121,211" come up

3. 1-OFF: 1-OFF allows you to still win a prize if the numbers that are drawn are either 1 number higher or lower than your selected numbers (example, you play the numbers 2-2-2, and the numbers 1-3-3 are drawn) (1-OFF is optional and costs $1.00). This amounts to an extra 26 ways to win, and changes the probability of winning a prize. In order to play it you must select the 1-OFF box on your lottery slip. You can be 1-OFF on either 1 or both, or all three numbers. Ask the Djinni about the games probability for in depth look at how the odds and probability off the 1-Off game work.
