History of the Djinn

Arabic collective noun deriving from the Semitic root jnn (Arabic: جِنّ‎, jann), meaning "to hide" or "to conceal". Some interpret it to mean beings concealed from the senses—also known as djinn, or genies. These supernatural entities appear across mythologies as spirits or demons.

We've taken the liberty of binding one to your device to help you make the most of your lottery experience.
